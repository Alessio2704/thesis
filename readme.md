## 📌 Executive Summary
This repository contains the complete thesis and research framework for a comprehensive approach to modern company valuation. Moving beyond traditional, static Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) models, this work bridges strict corporate finance with **Macroeconomic Theory**, **Geopolitical Risk Analysis**, **Institutional Economics**, and **Technological Infrastructure** to create a highly dynamic and multidisciplinary valuation model. 

The thesis explicitly stress-tests the DCF framework against severe exogenous shocks, demonstrating how to mathematically translate complex real-world events—from AI investment super-cycles to global trade wars—into precise financial inputs. Furthermore, it introduces advanced stochastic methodologies (Monte Carlo simulations and Algorithmic Differentiation) to transform valuation from a single-point estimate into a probabilistic topography of structural resilience.

This research serves as the theoretical and mathematical foundation for [**ValuaScript**](https://github.com/Alessio2704/valuascript-toolchain), a proprietary Domain-Specific Language (DSL) and C++20 compiler designed for high-performance financial simulations.

## 🔬 Core Research Pillars

### 1. Macroeconomic & Geopolitical Shock Integration
*   **Aggregate Demand & Trade Dynamics:** Modeling the direct DCF impact of government spending cycles (e.g., European Defense Industry rearmament), catastrophic demand destruction (e.g., Cruise lines during COVID-19), and supply chain fragility.
*   **Tariffs & Trade Wars:** Detailed forensic case studies on how aggressive trade policies (e.g., the 2025 US Tariff shift) mechanically compress margins, alter Total Addressable Market (TAM), and force supply chain reconfiguration (analyzed via Apple Inc.).
*   **Institutional Quality (AJR Framework):** Application of the Acemoglu-Johnson-Robinson framework to assess Country Risk Premiums (CRP). Analyzes how "extractive" institutions and geopolitical sanctions (e.g., the Russia case study) destroy equity value and create capital repatriation risks.

### 2. Technological Transition & Investment Cycles
*   **The Semiconductor Market (CISC vs. RISC):** A deep dive into the architectural war between **x86** and **ARM**. Explores how Apple's M-series silicon disrupted Intel's high-margin TAM, forcing capital-intensive strategic pivots.
*   **Artificial Intelligence Infrastructure:** Technoeconomic modeling of the AI investment cycle. Maps the flow of hyperscaler CapEx (AWS, GCP, Azure) to semiconductor designers (Nvidia) and foundry equipment suppliers (ASML), distinguishing between training and inference market economics.

### 3. Algorithmic Valuation Logic & Stochastic Extensions
*   **Pristine DCF Mechanics:** Standardization of complex adjustments, including Bottom-Up Beta estimation, R&D Capitalization, Operating Leases, and the bifurcation of Convertible Bonds.
*   **Monte Carlo Simulations:** Moving away from fragile, deterministic single-point inputs by utilizing empirical sampling distributions and probability density functions to model highly volatile variables (e.g., commodity prices).
*   **Algorithmic Differentiation (AD):** Leveraging advanced computational mathematics to calculate exact, simultaneous sensitivities for every model input, transforming the model into a real-time mathematical "heat map" for market analysis.
