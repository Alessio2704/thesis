\chapter*{Introduction}
The estimation of a company's intrinsic value represents a cornerstone activity in investment analysis, arguably paramount given its direct linkage to the monetary outcomes of investment decisions.\\
Determining this fair value, however, presents a complex challenge due to several factors: the necessity for financial modeling techniques with numerous parameters, the significant number and inherent subjectivity of required future estimates, and the constantly evolving macroeconomic and political landscape in which firms operate.
This thesis develops a framework for estimating company fair value, building upon the widely utilized Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model, by incorporating considerations from the political, macroeconomic, and firm-specific microeconomic environments into the valuation process.\\
The following thesis is structured as follows: the first part details the core valuation model itself, explaining the mathematical concepts and tools employed to arrive at the fair value determination. The second part examines how broader political and macroeconomic factors influence key valuation inputs and ultimately affect the final valuation estimate.
